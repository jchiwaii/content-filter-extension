Content Filter: Sentence Rewriting Test

Problem with asterisks: Your brain can easily auto-complete "This is ****ing great" to understand the original word. The context makes it obvious, defeating the purpose of filtering.

❌ Old Method (Asterisks)

Original:
This is fucking amazing!
Filtered:
This is ****ing amazing!
Issue: You can still read "****ing" as the original word. Your brain fills in the gap automatically.
Original:
What the hell is going on?
Filtered:
What the **** is going on?
Issue: The sentence structure still reveals the word. "What the ****" is obviously the original phrase.
Original:
That's damn good work.
Filtered:
That's **** good work.
Issue: Context makes it clear what the censored word is.

✅ New Method (Pattern Rewriting)

Original:
This is fucking amazing!
Rewritten:
This is very amazing!
Benefit: Natural-sounding sentence. No way to know what the original word was. The meaning is preserved.
Original:
What the hell is going on?
Rewritten:
What on earth is going on?
Benefit: Complete sentence rewrite. Sounds natural and conversational without revealing the original.
Original:
That's damn good work.
Rewritten:
That's very good work.
Benefit: Clean, professional sentence that maintains the positive sentiment.
How it works: The new system uses pattern-based rules to intelligently rewrite sentences:
  • "fucking/damn + adjective" → "very + adjective"
  • "what the fuck/hell" → "what on earth"
  • "as fuck" → "extremely"
  • "hell yeah/no" → "definitely yes/not"
  • And many more patterns...
If a word doesn't match any pattern, it's simply removed from the sentence.
